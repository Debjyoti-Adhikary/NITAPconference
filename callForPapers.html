Conference Topics


The CCITAS-2019 co-locates several symposia under one roof, each dedicated to a specific topic in the Computational and Communication Intelligence, thereby encouraging cross-fertilization of ideas and providing a unique platform for top researchers, professionals and students from all around the world to discuss and present their findings.

The technical program will consist of, but is not limited to, the following topics of interest:


  •    Adaptive Dynamic Programming and Reinforcement Learning
  •    Neural Network, Statistical, Multi-agent learning
  •    Online, incremental, scalability learning algorithm
  •    Machine learning for bioinformatics and computational biology
  •    Multi-lingual knowledge acquisition and representation
  •    Mathematical programming
  •    Knowledge discovery in databases
  •    Computational learning theory
  •    Cognitive modeling
  •    Hybrid learning algorithms
  •    Support Vector Machines (SVMs)
  •    Non-iterative and least square based models
  •    Big Data, Deep Learning, IoT
  •    Communication and Networking
  •    Software Define Networking
  •    Security & Privacy in Wired, Wireless, Mobile, Hybrid, Sensor, Ad Hoc networks
  •    Communication Privacy and Anonymity
  •    Network Intrusion Detection and Prevention, Firewalls, Packet Filters
  •    Public Key Infrastructures, key management, credentials


Applications:


  •    Medicine, Health, Bioinformatics and Systems Biology
  •    Detection, prediction, analysis, and classification of human brain, heart, and other disorders
  •    Industrial and Engineering applications
  •    Wireless System and Security applications
  •    Control and Automation Systems
  •    Security and Defense Applications
  •    Smart cities
  •    Cyber Security, Dynamic and Uncertain Environments
  •    Remote Sensing
  •    Game playing and problem solving
  •    Intelligent virtual environments
  •    Economics, Business and forecasting applications, etc
  •    Applications related to machine leaning and signal processing
  •    Water engineering
